## Call for Papers

### Status
"The call for papers is now closed -- thanks to everyone who submitted!"

### Submission Overview
The workshop invited short and long papers due **May 8th, 2026 (AOE)**. Both ICML and NeurIPS formats were accepted, though camera-ready versions required ICML formatting. Submissions should demonstrate how they advance mechanistic interpretability research.

**Submission Platform:** OpenReview (https://openreview.net/group?id=ICML.cc/2026/Workshop/Mech_Interp)

### Reviewer Requirements
Each submission required at least one reciprocal reviewer. Due to "x2.6 the number of submissions from last year," each reviewer was assigned 4 papers instead of the original 3. Individuals could serve as reciprocal reviewers on a maximum of 3 papers.

### Key Details

**Workshop Nature:**
- Non-archival event
- Acceptance notifications by **June 12th (AOE)**

**Submission Eligibility:**
- Papers already accepted to ICML 2026 welcome (fast-track option available)
- Papers accepted to other archival venues NOT accepted
- Prior 2025 workshop acceptances will not be reconsidered
- Previously rejected 2025 submissions without meaningful revisions face desk rejection
- Submissions under peer review elsewhere are welcome

**Format Requirements:**
- Use ICML 2026 LaTeX Template or NeurIPS 2026 template
- Short papers: 4 pages (ICML) or 5 pages (NeurIPS)
- Long papers: 8 pages (ICML) or 9 pages (NeurIPS)
- References and appendices unlimited but not expected to be read
- Accepted papers receive one additional page for camera-ready revisions
- All final submissions must use ICML format

**Technical Requirements:**
- All submissions via OpenReview
- Note: Non-institutional emails may take up to 2 weeks for account approval
- Double-blind review process requires anonymization of identifying details

### Reproducibility and Openness
Authors encouraged to open-source code, models, prompts, data, and interactive demonstrations. Reviewers specifically assess reproducibility.

**Recommended Platforms:**
- Anonymous GitHub sharing: https://anonymous.4open.science/
- Model weights/datasets: Anonymous HuggingFace accounts
- Interactive demos: Anonymous websites or Streamlit apps

### Accepted Work Categories

Beyond standard empirical research, the workshop welcomes:

**Alternative Research Formats:**
- "Rigorous negative results"
- "Rigorous replications of important results"
- Critiques or failed replications
- Open-source software (examples: TransformerLens, nnsight, pyvene, Penzai)
- Community tools (Neuronpedia, Docent)
- Research models/datasets (Pythia, MultiBERTs, Gemma Scope)
- Educational materials
- Concept distillations
- Position pieces clarifying complex debates

**Empirical Work Standards:**
Strong empirical submissions must either: (i) "clearly articulate specific falsifiable hypotheses, and how the evidence provided does and does not support them" OR (ii) demonstrate "clear practical benefits over well-implemented baselines."

Papers documenting both strengths and limitations are welcomed even with inconclusive results. Works downplaying limitations will be rejected.

## Topics of Interest

### Understanding Model Internals
- Learning about high-level properties (representations, feature geometry) and internal usage
- Evidence for cognitive phenomena (latent reasoning, implicit planning, search algorithms, world models)
- Representation of beliefs, personas, world models, reasoning processes, implicit goals

### Methods for Mechanistic Discovery
- Uncovering and validating internal structures
- Improvements to circuit analyses, causal methods, attribution graphs, dictionary learning, training-data attributions
- Novel paradigms for discovery

### Practical Applications, Development, and Benchmarking
- Interpretability tools for real-world applications, model development, evaluations
- Identifying, debugging, and fixing undesirable behaviors
- Progress benchmarking methodologies

### Safety, Monitoring, and Model Repair
- Developing safer models through interpretability
- Detecting alignment faking, subliminal learning, emergent misalignment
- Monitoring deployed models

### Scaling and Automation
- Expanding beyond controlled domains to realistic settings on frontier models
- Generalizing insights from toy settings
- Automating interpretability efforts (agents, Activation Oracle-style decoding)

### Domain-Specific Foundation Models
- Reverse-engineering and understanding foundation models across domains

### Conceptual and Foundational Work
- Frameworks for characterizing model internals
- Definitions of concepts like "features" and "circuits"
